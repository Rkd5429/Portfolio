For this project, I’ve decided to work with two authors who have mainly done horror element stories.

I’ve used two short stories by Edgar Allan Poe (The Tell-Tale Heart and The Cask of Amontillado) and one novel by Stephen King (The Shining).

What I'm hoping to gather from both of these authors when I compare them is the differences in writing, the way characters refer to each other, and the

way the “horror” aspect happens within the text.


On the left side, I have The Shining.

On the right side, I have The Tell Tale Heart and The Cask of Amontillado


The Shining

The Shining was filled to the brim with stop words such as “a,” “the,” “is,” “are”. Those very same words were placed as the most occuring words within the text which to me is problematic. The Word "a" for example had a staggering count of roughly 3800 times. Without even writting down the amount for each of those stop words we cam already assume that the most written words were in fact stop words. So I decided to purge many of those words until more interesting words could be found.


Now that the annoying words have been purged, I can finally see the more important words appear with the now highest word count.


Word Count

  • Danny = 917
  • Jack = 750
  • Been = 678
  • There = 655
  • Like = 648


The N-Grams that I found from The Shining were not quite what I expected, although maybe I should have. For the N-grams, I never actually used any of the common words when finding them, meaning I just found the most common sentences in general based off of nothing but the text itself. For now I'll use the N-Grams that have any interesting details instead of the top five.


N-Grams

I looked at two clusters within Antconc for The Shining and decided to use both Jack and Danny for them.




The clusters told me a small amount of information about the two characters. For starters, while the first cluster doesnt quite say so but the second one has a lot of information that suggests that Danny and Jack are typically seen together within the story. The Cask of Amontillado

The Cask of Amontillado surprised me with the lack of stop words. It still had a mass amount of them, but significantly less than The Tell Tale Heart and especially The Shining. The most used word outside of stop words was found an astounding 24 times.

Word Count

  • Said = 24
  • Amontillado = 21
  • Ugh = 15
  • Fortunato = 14
  • Replied = 8


The N-Grams for The Cask of Amontillado have been amusing with first N-Gram being "ugh ugh ugh ugh". This was used within the text a total of twelve times. Beyond seeing that without context, I think that it actually fits well into what the horror element of what this short story could be. The other N-Grams that were worth mentioning made it seem as if ghosts or some supernatural entity were the main element. It even further pushes that thought when we see the word "catacombs".


N-Grams
